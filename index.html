üö© Module 2: Understanding Online Predators

Let's understand how predators operate online and what techniques they use to target teens.

üì± Real Story: Emma's Experience

"I was 14 when someone claiming to be a 16-year-old girl from another school friended me. We talked for weeks about music and movies. Then she started asking for personal photos. When I refused, she got angry. My mom helped me report the account, which turned out to be a 42-year-old man."

What warning signs did you spot?

  • Requesting personal photos
  • Getting upset when boundaries are set
  • Creating a false identity to seem relatable

Common Tactics Used by Online Predators:

  • Fake Profiles - Creating personas that appeal to teens (similar age, interests)
  • Flattery - Excessive compliments about appearance or talents
  • Isolation - Trying to move conversations to private platforms
  • Testing Boundaries - Gradually introducing inappropriate topics

üß† Module 3: What is Grooming?

Grooming is a process predators use to gain trust and manipulate young people. Let's learn to recognize it.

The 5 Stages of Grooming

1. Targeting the Victim

Predators look for vulnerable teens who post about feeling lonely, fighting with parents, or seeking validation.

2. Gaining Trust

They build rapport through shared interests, offering understanding, and making the teen feel special.

3. Filling Needs

They provide emotional support, gifts, attention, or affirmation that the teen may crave.

4. Isolating/Desensitizing

They create a "special relationship" while gradually introducing inappropriate content or requests.

5. Maintaining Control

They use guilt, threats, or manipulation to keep the teen engaged and secretive about the relationship.
